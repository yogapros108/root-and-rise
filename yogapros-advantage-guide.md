## TWO MODELS OF QUALITY ASSURANCE

Before we show you what YogaPros gives you, you need to understand how we think about quality — because it is fundamentally different from every other accreditation body.

### THE TRADITIONAL MODEL: Inspect and Police

Some people say accreditation bodies should send assessors to every course to inspect standards. Think about what that actually means:

- **Financially unviable** — sending assessors to courses in 100+ countries would require enormous fees, passed on to trainers and students
- **Professionally insulting** — a Senior Yoga Teacher with 8,000+ hours would not accept an individual assessor questioning their professional judgement on whether to pass or fail a graduate
- **Episodic** — checked once, forgotten until renewal
- **Gameable** — schools prepare for inspection day, then revert
- **Adversarial** — creates an us-vs-them dynamic between body and trainer
- **No feedback loop** — poor outcomes stay invisible between inspections

### THE YOGAPROS MODEL: Design for Quality Outcomes

We set the bar high at the gate, then create systems where quality is continuous, transparent, and self-correcting.

- **Continuous** — quality signals flowing all the time, not once a year
- **Transparent** — graduate reviews visible to everyone
- **Collaborative** — trainers and graduates grow together
- **Self-correcting** — poor outcomes surface naturally through public data

This is not softer. It is smarter. And it is why our standards are the highest in the industry.

## SEVEN QUALITY MECHANISMS THAT PROTECT YOUR REPUTATION

YogaPros accreditation is not a rubber stamp. It is a system designed so quality is continuous, transparent, and self-correcting. Seven mechanisms working together.

### MECHANISM 1: The Highest Gatekeeper Standards in the Industry

The lead trainer on any YogaPros-accredited Foundation course must be a Senior Yoga Teacher — minimum 8 years and 4,000 hours of teaching experience. They must personally deliver at least 70% of the 180 minimum contact hours.

This single requirement eliminates the biggest risk in teacher training: inexperienced trainers producing unready graduates.

**Compare:** Yoga Alliance USA requires just 4 years / 2,000 hours for lead trainers, and allows "Other Faculty" (non-registered trainers) to teach without equivalent experience requirements.

### MECHANISM 2: Verified Graduate Reviews

Every graduate from a YogaPros-accredited course can leave a review of their training — they do not need to join YogaPros to do this. We verify that the reviewer actually completed the course.

Five questions, but two matter most:

> ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **"Do you feel confident and ready to teach?"**

> ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **"Would you work with this trainer again?"**

A trainer who consistently produces graduates who do not feel ready to teach becomes visible — no inspector needed. The reviews are public and build over time on the trainer's Brand Profile.

**Self-correcting — quality surfaces naturally.**

### MECHANISM 3: Pre-Course Assessment

Every YogaPros-accredited training school must run an effective pre-course assessment — ideally live and in person. This is a two-way process:

**For the trainer:** Ensure the right students are accepted on the course — the right level of practice, the right attitude, and realistic expectations.

**For the student:** A chance to meet the trainer, experience their teaching style, and assess whether this course is the right fit — before committing time and money.

Two-way assessment means better fit, fewer dropouts, and higher-quality outcomes for everyone.

### MECHANISM 4: First-Year Graduate Support

The training environment and the real world are very different. A controlled classroom with supportive peers is nothing like walking into a studio full of strangers with injuries, expectations, and questions you did not cover in training.

We ask trainers to support their graduate teachers through their first year of teaching. This is a win for everyone:

| Who Benefits | How |
|---|---|
| **New Teacher** | Real-world guidance when they need it most. Confidence to teach safely from day one. |
| **Their Students** | A better-prepared teacher who has ongoing support and mentorship behind them. |
| **The Trainer** | Real-world data on what new teachers actually face. Keeps courses relevant and up to date. |

The trainer can offer additional training (paid or free) to address real-world gaps, update their course content based on actual feedback, and build a lifelong relationship with their graduate teachers. Graduates become raving fans — and leave more reviews on the trainer's Brand Profile and Live CV.

### MECHANISM 5: Practice Requirements

All trainees should have a minimum of two years of strong, regular practice before entering teacher training. They must be able to demonstrate postures to an acceptable level for teaching.

This is not about being the most flexible person in the room. It is about having enough embodied experience to teach safely, adapt to different bodies, and know the difference between a posture that works and one that does not.

**Teach from experience — not theory.**

### MECHANISM 6: Annual Trainer Reviews

Most accreditation bodies do a one-off check — a snapshot in time — then leave trainers alone until renewal. We review every trainer account and their events annually, checking for changes in standards, delivery, and compliance.

This is not an inspection. It is an ongoing relationship. Combined with continuous graduate reviews, it means quality is monitored year-round — not just on the day someone applies for accreditation.

**Continuous oversight — not a snapshot in time.**

### MECHANISM 7: Non-Registered Trainer Requirements

We recognise that yoga teacher training benefits from specialist knowledge — anatomy, physiology, business, mental health. Non-yoga specialists can teach up to 30% of the course content.

But they must have a minimum of 4 years and 2,000 hours of experience in their chosen field. The remaining 70% or more must be delivered by the Senior Yoga Teacher lead trainer.

**Compare:** Yoga Alliance USA allows "Other Faculty" (non-registered trainers) to teach on courses with no equivalent experience requirement.

## DEVELOPMENT TRAINING: EXPAND YOUR REVENUE

Already running CPD workshops or specialist training? Accredit those too. Each accredited course is another revenue stream, another set of verified reviews, and another reason students find you.

### A CRITICAL DISTINCTION ON PRE-RECORDED CONTENT

**Foundation Training (training new teachers from scratch):** Pre-recorded content is never accepted. Zero percent. You cannot learn to teach yoga from a video. 180 minimum contact hours with a Senior Yoga Teacher who has 4,000+ hours of experience. No shortcuts.

**Development Training (qualified professionals doing CPD):** Pre-recorded content is accepted for existing qualified teachers — just as a doctor watches pre-recorded lectures for CPD. The critical difference: assessment is always live, in person or via Zoom. You prove competence face to face.

This is the standard in every profession. We are transparent about the distinction because we have nothing to hide.

### REGULAR DEVELOPMENT TRAINING

- For existing qualified yoga teachers (further training / CPD)
- Minimum 28 hours total
- Pre-recorded content accepted with live assessment
- Lead trainer: EYT or equivalent (minimum 4 years / 2,000 hours)
- For expanding skills, deepening knowledge, maintaining professional edge

### SPECIALIST DEVELOPMENT TRAINING (Higher Risk)

- Higher-risk specialist categories requiring hands-on competence
- Minimum 28 contact hours, 40 total hours
- Must be delivered live (in person or online live) — no pre-recorded content
- Lead trainer: EYT or equivalent (minimum 4 years / 2,000 hours in their specialist topic)
- Earns a verified specialty badge on the graduate's Live CV
- Only fully qualified teachers who complete this training can claim these specialties
