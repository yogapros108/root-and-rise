Two types of development

Regular and Specialist — different standards for different risks

Not all further training carries the same risk. A breathwork workshop is different from pregnancy yoga training. The standards reflect that distinction.

Continuing professional development

Further training to deepen your skills, expand your knowledge, and maintain your professional edge. Workshops, intensives, and CPD courses for qualified teachers.

Specialist Development

Higher-risk specialist training

Training in specialist categories that require hands-on competence. These carry higher risk — pregnancy, trauma, kids, cancer — and earn a verified specialty badge on your Live CV.

  • Earns a verified specialty badge on your Live CV
  • Only qualified teachers can claim these specialties

An important distinction

Pre-recorded content: why Development is different from Foundation

This is one of the most misunderstood areas in yoga training accreditation. Here's the clear distinction:

Foundation Training

Pre-recorded content: never accepted

You cannot learn to teach yoga from a video. Foundation training requires 180 minimum contact hours with a Senior Yoga Teacher who has 8,000+ hours of experience. There are no shortcuts to becoming a qualified teacher.

Development Training

Pre-recorded content: accepted for qualified teachers

You're already a qualified professional. You can learn new material through pre-recorded content — just as a doctor watches pre-recorded lectures for CPD. The difference: assessment is always live (in person or via Zoom). You prove competence face to face. The exception: Specialist categories are always delivered live — the risk demands it.

Before you invest

Questions to ask any development course

If a course carries the YogaPros Development badge, you already know the answers. If it doesn't, ask them.

Ask this

"How many years of experience does the trainer have in this specific topic?"

YogaPros accredited: minimum 4 years / 2,000 hours in the subject. Not general yoga — the actual specialty they're teaching you.

Ask this

"How is assessment conducted?"

YogaPros accredited: always live. In person or Zoom. A real person watches you demonstrate competence. If it's a multiple-choice quiz, ask yourself if that's enough.

Ask this

"Can I speak to teachers who've completed this course?"

YogaPros accredited: yes — DM any reviewer directly through the platform. No trainer involvement. If they only offer curated testimonials, why?

Ask this

"Will this qualification be verified and visible to employers?"

YogaPros accredited: yes — recorded on your Live CV and verifiable by anyone. If the answer is "you get a PDF certificate," consider how much that's worth.

Ask this

"Is this course accredited by an independent body?"

YogaPros accredited: yes — independently verified by the global professional body. If the answer is "self-certified" or "we've applied," there's a reason they don't have it yet.

Ask this

"For specialist training — is it delivered live?"

YogaPros accredited specialist: 100% live delivery. Hands-on competence can't be assessed from a video. If they're teaching pregnancy yoga through pre-recorded content, that should concern you.
